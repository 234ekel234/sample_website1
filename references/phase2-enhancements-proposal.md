**Subject:** PMAFI Website — Optional Phase 2 Enhancements

Dear DCD,

With the website live, I wanted to share a short menu of **optional enhancements**
we could take on in a second phase. None of these are required — the current
site stands on its own — but each one makes the website do more for the
Foundation automatically: handling payments, letting members serve themselves,
and letting your staff manage content without a developer.

These are listed roughly in order of impact. We can do one, several, or none,
and space them out however suits the Foundation's budget.

### 1. Online Payments — *biggest impact*
Accept **donations and membership dues online** via GCash, Maya, or card
(through a Philippine payment gateway such as PayMongo or Xendit), with automatic
receipts. Today the flow is manual — apply, we email an invoice, the member sends
proof, staff update the record. This closes the loop end-to-end and makes giving
effortless, which typically increases contributions.
- **Value:** turns the site from informational into a system that collects money.
- **Needs from PMAFI:** a merchant/business account with the gateway (verification).
- **Indicative range:** ₱25,000 – ₱45,000

### 2. Staff Admin Dashboard
A private dashboard where PMAFI staff **manage the member roster and confirm
payments** directly — no editing a spreadsheet by hand. Mark members Active,
review applications, see who's pending.
- **Value:** removes day-to-day dependence on the developer; fewer errors.
- **Indicative range:** ₱20,000 – ₱35,000

### 3. Member Self-Service Area
A **member login** where members view their own status, download receipts, and
access members-only resources or announcements.
- **Value:** a real members' benefit; reduces inquiries to staff.
- **Indicative range:** ₱20,000 – ₱40,000

### 4. Content Management (self-editing)
Let staff **edit News, the Chairman's message, programs, and board details**
themselves — no developer needed for routine updates. There are two ways we can
do this, and the choice sets where in the range the cost lands:
- **Simple & free (recommended to start):** content is managed in a **Google
  Sheet**, exactly like the member roster already works today — no new tool,
  no new login, no added cost. Best for news entries and short text.
- **Full editor:** a proper content dashboard (e.g. Sanity) with **rich text
  and drag-and-drop image uploads** for non-technical staff. A little more setup
  (and a new login), typically still on a free tier.
- **Value:** keeps the site current; saves recurring change requests to me.
- **Indicative range:** ₱15,000 – ₱30,000 *(lower end = Google Sheet approach,
  higher end = full editor)*

### 5. Automated Emails
Automatic **invoice on application, donation/dues receipts, and renewal
reminders**, in PMAFI's name.
- **Value:** professional, consistent, and hands-off.
- **Needs from PMAFI:** the dues/invoice rules and email content.
- **Indicative range:** ₱10,000 – ₱20,000

### 6. Transparency / Reports Page
A page for **financial reports, board resolutions, and official documents** — a
strong trust signal for a foundation that solicits donations (PMAAAI does this).
- **Value:** donor confidence.
- **Needs from PMAFI:** the documents to publish.
- **Indicative range:** ₱5,000 – ₱10,000

### 7. Analytics & Search Visibility
**Google Analytics** for visitor insight, plus structured data so PMAFI appears
better in Google results (organization details, rich listings).
- **Value:** see what's working; easier to find online.
- **Indicative range:** ₱3,000 – ₱8,000

---

**My recommendation:** if the Foundation pursues a second phase, start with
**#1 (Online Payments)** and **#2 (Staff Dashboard)** together — that pair
delivers the most value, completing the membership and donation flow end-to-end.
The rest can follow whenever it makes sense.

Happy to scope any of these in detail or walk through them on a call. No

## Notes (for Simoun — not part of the proposal)

  features would lower the combined price. Adjust to your comfort.

  business verification; flag this early as it can take time.
- **Sequencing logic:** payments + admin dashboard reinforce each other (collect
  money → manage it). CMS (#4) pays off most if PMAFI updates content often.
- **Positioning:** these justify moving toward the agency tier (₱110k–₱250k for
  the whole platform) while still being delivered at a fraction of that.
