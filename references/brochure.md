PMA: Molding leaders for the nation
The Philippine Military Academy (PMA) is an officer-candidate training institution for the Armed Forces of the Philippines.

The mission of the PMA is "to instruct, train and develop the cadets, so that each graduate shall possess the character, the broad and basic military skills, and the education essential to the successful pursuit of a progressive military career, as a valued committed leader in selfless service to the AFP and the nation."

PMAFI: Partnering in developing future leaders
The Philippine Military Academy Foundation, Inc. (PMAFI) is a non-stock, non-profit corporation registered with the SEC in 1988. Its purpose, in general, is to contribute to scholastic progress and to national development. Specifically, the PMAFI was established to help the PMA improve the quality of its instruction and make it a center for academic excellence and character development.

Organized for effectiveness and efficiency
The affairs and business of the PMAFI are managed by a 15-man Board of Trustees, elected from among its regular members.

Middle Column
The PMA Superintendent is an ex-officio member of the Board.

The day-to-day activities of the Foundation are handled by the President who exercises general supervision and direction of its affairs and business. The President is assisted by the Vice-President and other officers. The trustees and officers do not receive any compensation for their services.

Committed membership
Membership in the PMAFI is categorized as regular, associate or affiliate. Regular and associate members come from the alumni, faculty and staff of the PMA. Affiliate membership, on the other hand, is open to selected persons and entities who share the core values and support the vision, mission and objectives of the PMAFI.

As of 28 February 2012, the PMAFI has 3,110 regular, 32 associate and 229 affiliate members.

Helping PMA provide quality education and training
Through the years, the PMAFI has remained a reliable partner of the PMA in providing the cadets with competent and dedicated military and civilian instructors.

To sustain competence among the teaching staff, the PMAFI implements the Academy's Faculty Development Program by... (continued in the next column)

Right Column
(...continued from the middle column) ...way of scholarship grants for graduate and postgraduate studies, professorial chair and research grants to underwrite specialists' studies, grant teaching and course development, and seminar grants to keep the instructors abreast with the latest trends in teaching while updating their knowledge on the various courses that they are handling.

Additionally, the PMAFI assists in upgrading the PMA training facilities, like the classrooms, lecture rooms, and computer and physics laboratories, for a more conducive learning environment and easy access to information. The PMAFI also contributes to the PMA Library Modernization Program by way of donating newly published books and reference materials on a regular basis.

Likewise, state-of-the-art training equipment are provided to give the cadets and their instructors the opportunity to work with the latest equipment available for quality instruction.

Finally, to inspire the instructors to continuously provide the cadets with high quality instruction, the PMAFI confers Teaching Excellence and Academic Excellence Awards to outstanding faculty members from each department.

Investing in human capital
The PMA is a prime investment in the training of leaders of character. For the past 24 years, the PMAFI has provided ₱62.34 million worth of assistance to PMA.

Professorial chair and endowment funds
The PMAFI is being supported by alumni and friends of the Academy through membership contributions, donation in the form of Professorial Chair and Endowment Funds, and fundraising activities.

A professorial chair, established in the name of the donor, may be endowed with a minimum amount of ₱250,000. An endowment fund may also be put up in an amount of at least ₱100,000. The principal of these funds will not be spent; only the earnings will be used to support the project or the recipient of the grant.

Tax-exempt donor institution
The PMAFI is registered with the BIR as a tax-exempt donor institution and is qualified as a donee institution under Section 30(H) of the Tax Reform Act of 1997. Hence, donations made to PMAFI are fully tax-deductible by the donor.
